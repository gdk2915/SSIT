All about Cattle feed business

India is the highest producer of milk with 187.7 million tonnes production in 2018-19. India also boasts about the highest no of cattle in the world. (National Dairy Development Board) However, themilk produced per animal is only about 987 L per lactation vs global average of 2200 L per lactation i.e. only about 40%. The main reason for this difference is non-scientific feeding practices, inappropriate feed quality and quantity,affordability of quality feed and fodder.

The nutritional quality as well as quantity of the cattle feed is directly responsible for milk yield from the cattle. The feed given to cattle is used primarily for maintenance and self-growth. Next, it is used for the growth of the foetus in pregnant cattle. Only after these needs are fulfilled the surplus feed is used for milk production. Thus, to obtain maximum milk from the cattle it is necessary to feed maximally to the fulfil basic requirements and then produce milk.

An ideal cattle feed provides energy, protein, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins, minerals, fibre and water in appropriate amounts. A balanced combination of all these components is important to get maximum milk productivity. Any component which is lacking or available in lesser quantities becomes limiting to the animal resulting in reduced productivity of milk.

Although some of the components are obtained from natural ingredients normally fed to the cattle (e.g. Dollied Cakes, Chunnis, corn, bran etc.) many a times some components such as total proteins, certain amino acids vitamins and minerals become limiting and hence need to be provided additionally in the form of concentrates to ensure nutrition fulfilment . In short, any feed mix based on natural ingredients contains some required components in limiting amounts thus making the feed nutritionally unbalanced.

These observations lead to the concept of concentrated feed (or feed concentrates). According to AAFCO a feed concentrate is a feed used with regular feed to improve the nutritive balance of the total ration and intended to be further mixed to produce a supplement or a complete feed . (AAFCO, 2000).

Feed concentrates provide the essential limiting nutrients in required amounts thus balancing the ratio of various components and in very small volumes and hence can be easily added to the regular ration without compromising the total feeding needs of the cattle. An intelligent selection of the limiting components for making the feed concentrate is essential to make better feed concentrate. Use of a high-quality feed concentrate results in extended production of milk by the cattle thus leading to obtaining maximum output from the animal at minimal incremental cost.

In general, the milking efficiency is calculated by milk obtained between two lactations cycles. In Indian varieties of cattle, the calving interval generally is 12 to 13 months, with a lactation length of about 300 days, followed by a 60 to 90-day dry period.

In Indian scenario, post calving, the milk production peaks at around 70 days. After that the milk productivity reduces linearly up to 180-250 days (6-8 months) after which the milk production is minimal diverting the nutrition for the growth of foetus and dry milking periods in pregnant cattle. Thus, the dry period extends up to 120-150 days. The nutritional requirement in each stage is unique and needs to be addressed correctly to ensure highest productivity of milk in the next milking cycle.
